What does it mean by being sustainable?

Being renewable is not enough. Energy needs to be sustainable. Sustainability is determined by three different parameters: environmental sustainability, social sustainability and economic sustainability. Environmental sustainability means that it doesn’t do harm to the environment. Social sustainability means society, or any social system, should persistently achieve a good social well being. Economic sustainability requires that a business or country uses its resources efficiently and responsibly so that it can operate in a sustainable manner to consistently produce an operational profit.

Some sustainability goals:

Reduce sanitary waste from routine operations by 25 percent by next year, using a 2005 baseline.

Recycle 45 percent of sanitary wastes from all operations by next year and 50 percent in 5 years.

Reduce fleet petroleum consumption by 20 percent by next year, using a 2000 baseline.

Ensure that 75 percent of new light-duty vehicles purchased each year are alternative-fuel vehicles.

What you can do in your daily life to save energy:

Turn down your thermostat at night or when you’re away for more than four hours during the day. Do not turn off your heating system entirely as this may cause pipes to freeze.

Use Energy Star LED or compact fluorescent light bulbs. Energy Star light bulbs last longer and use up to 80 percent less energy than standard light bulbs.

Laser printers use an incredible amount of energy. Switch to ink-jet printers.

Don’t over-cool. The ideal thermostat setting is between 75°F and 78°F. Every degree you raise your thermostat can result in a 5% savings on the cost of cooling your home.

Set the refrigerator thermostat at 38 degrees F for fresh food compartments and 5 degrees F for the freezer compartment
